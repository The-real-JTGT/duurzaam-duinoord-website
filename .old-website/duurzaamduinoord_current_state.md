
# Duurzaam Duinoord Website – Current State Documentation

https://www.duurzaamduinoord.nl as observed during analysis. The goal of this
document is to provide a **complete reference of the existing site** so that the
content can be reused when building a new website.

The document contains:

- Site structure / organisation chart
- Purpose of each page
- Visible information on each page
- Forms and calls‑to‑action
- Recurring footer elements
- Notes about incomplete CMS content and placeholders

# 4. Energiecoaching

Purpose:
Provide free energy coaching to residents.

Visible information:

Residents can ask questions about making their home more sustainable,
available options, installers, or subsidies.

Energy coaches:
- Live in the neighbourhood
- Know the housing types
- Are trained volunteers
- Provide independent advice

Services:
Home visits where coaches review the situation and suggest measures.

The coaching is available for:
- Homeowners
- Tenants
- Homeowners associations (VvE)

Project example:
Street‑based approach in Voltastraat (Sept 2022 – end of 2023).

Goal:
Involve residents in the energy transition and help them take sustainability
measures.

CTA:
Request a free energy coach conversation.

# 5. Warmtetransitie Duinoord

Purpose:
Explain the transition away from natural gas.

Main points:

The Netherlands is transitioning away from gas for:
- Heating
- Cooking
- Hot water

Most homes in the district still use gas.

The Wet Collectieve Warmtevoorziening gives municipalities responsibility for
the heat transition.

The Hague aims to become gas‑free by 2050.

Challenges:
Electricity grid congestion due to:
- Heat pumps
- Solar panels
- Electric vehicles

Likely direction for Duinoord:
A district heating network.

Temperature range:
60–80°C.

# 7. Warmtebeelden

Purpose:
Offer free thermal imaging of houses.

Explanation:

Thermal images show where facades lose heat.

Colours indicate temperature differences:
- Blue/green = colder
- Yellow/red = warmer

Red areas indicate potential heat loss.

Residents receive images by email.

# 13. Duinoord onder de Kurk

Purpose:
Research project on cork‑sedum roofs.

Research period:
Late 2023 – April 2025.

Objectives:
Measure insulation and cooling effects.

Installation details:

Gravel roof removed (5 cm layer).

Sedum‑cork cassettes installed:
Size:
50 × 33 cm.

Weight:
Maximum 51 kg per m².

Sensors installed to monitor temperatures.
